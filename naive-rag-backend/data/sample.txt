Retrieval-Augmented Generation (RAG) is a powerful approach that combines information retrieval with language models to provide accurate, grounded responses.

RAG systems work by retrieving relevant documents or passages from a knowledge base and using them as context for the language model to generate responses. This approach significantly improves the quality and relevance of generated content.

Key benefits of RAG systems include:
1. Improved accuracy through grounding in real documents
2. Reduced hallucination by using retrieved facts
3. Better handling of domain-specific queries
4. More transparent and verifiable responses

The RAG pipeline typically consists of three main components:
- Document Store: Stores and indexes documents for efficient retrieval
- Retriever: Finds relevant documents based on semantic similarity
- Generator: Uses retrieved documents to generate the final response

RAG systems have applications in question-answering, customer support, document analysis, and many other domains.
